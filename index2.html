Dr. Maria Skłodowska-Curie

Love, life and radioactivity

Dr. Norman Borlaug, second from left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a time line of Maria's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Kingdom of Poland (part of the Russian Empire at the time).
  • 1876 - Death of Maria's sister: Zosia
  • 1877 - Starts school
  • 1878 - Death of Maria's mother
  • 1883 - Finishes gymnasium achieving gold medal
  • 1883/1884 - One year spent with five different relatives at the countryside

"I was only fifteen when I finished my high-school studies, always having held first rank in my class. The fatigue of growth and study compelled me to take almost a year's rest in the country. I then returned to my father in Warsaw, hoping to teach in the free schools."

Marie Curie
  • 1884 - Starts Flying University (named as it in 1886)
  • 1885-1989 - Bronisława (Maria's sister) goes to Paris to study. Maria stays in Poland and works to support her sister. After Bronisława finishes, she will support Maria.
  • 1890 -1891 - Gets access to the laboratory in the Museum of Industry and Agriculture; learns chemical lab procedures
  • 1891 - Moves to Paris
  • 1991 - Starts studying at Sorbonne, as one of 3 women (and 1825 men)
  • 1893 - Recives Licenciate in Science (1st place)
  • 1983 - Recives scholarship for Poles studying abroad and gets hired by "Towarzystwo Popierania Przemysłu Krajowego" to study magnetic properties of different metals and metal steel
  • 1984 - Meets Pierre Curie

"I met Pierre Curie for the first time in the spring of the year 1894... A Polish physicist whom I knew, and who was a great admirer of Pierre Curie, one day invited us together to spend the evening with himself and his wife."

Marie Curie
  • 1894 - Recives Licenciate in Mathematics (2nd place)
  • 1894 - Returns to Poland. Tries to get a place at a university but Polish people are limited in what they are allowed to study in the Russian Empire, and women are not allowed to study at all. Convienced by her father returns to Paris. (find info)
  • 1985 - Marries Pierre.

"Pierre Curie came to see me and showed a simple and sincere sympathy with my student life. Soon he caught the habit of speaking to me of his dream of an existence consecrated entirely to scientific research, and he asked me to share that life."

Marie Curie
  • 1897 - Finishes studies on steel and decides on a topic for doctoral studies
  • 1898 - Maria and Pierre discover a new element and name it polonium after Poland
  • 1898 - Maria and Pierre discover radium; name formed in Modern Latin from radius (ray): this was in recognition of radium's power of emitting energy in the form of rays: this was in recognition of radium's power of emitting energy in the form of rays.
  • 1902 - Maria and Pierre isolate 1/10 miligram of radium and are able to measure it's weight
  • 1903 - Maria, Pierre, and Henri Becquerel receive the Nobel Prize. Neither Maria nor Pierre were able to travel to receive the award in person

"When radium was discovered, no one knew that it would prove useful in hospitals. The work was one of pure science. And this is a proof that scientific work must not be considered from the point of view of the direct usefulness of it."

Marie Curie
  • 1906 - Pierre dies in an accident. Maria starts writing detaild daily journal.
  • 1906 - Takes over Pierres position at Sorbonne
  • 1910 - Applies to the French Academy of Sciences. She was rejected. The press was very anti-Polish, anti-Slavic, and anti-Jewish (Maria was not Jewish, but she was an atheist, and that for many was a proof enough that she must be Jewish) as well as anti-woman. She never reapplied.
  • 1911 - Recives 2nd Nobel Prize.
  • 1914 - Gets involved in operating x-rey mobile units during WWI. Due to her determination during 4 years of WWI, 20 mobile cars were used and 1,000,000 soldiers were helped, most surviving the injuries.
  • 1921 - Visit in USA. Receives 1g of radium bough with money collected my American Women.
  • 1925 - Initiate building Polish Radium Institute in Warsaw.
  • 1928 - Second US visit. Aimed at buying radium for the Warsaw institute. Mission accomplished.
  • 1932 - Travels to Warsaw for the opening of the Polish Radium Institute.
  • 1934 - Dies. She worked until the very last days.

"You cannot hope to build a better world without improving the individuals. To that end, each of us must work for his own improvement and, at the same time, share a general responsibility for all humanity, our particular duty being to aid those to whom we think we can be most useful."

Marie Curie
